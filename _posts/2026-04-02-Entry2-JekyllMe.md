# Installing Jekyll(Me)
Today I learned a lesson. A short one, but a good one. Let me take you through my process of installing jekyll.

The first step was to look up how to install jekyll. Success, I found a hundred different tutorials, so there must be something in there for me!

Turns out that something having a hundred different tutorials **is not a good sign.**

> Whenever there are a million ways to do something, I tend to get a little spooked.
> I like paying attention to the details and picking just the right resource for my use-case, but it's
> often incredibly hard to find someone on the internet saying *"Hey it just so happens that I'm doing this thing you are also trying."*
> 
> Hardware and software is also often a questionmark. *Will this resource work on my lenovo laptop with windows 11 but using WSL but I don't eat lasagna??*
> 
> Tutorial makers have it difficult, trying to find a balance between simplicity and making sure anyone can use it, whatever their tools.
> I do believe that the world of IT has made it very hard for themselves. 
> 
>**Too many different standards, versions, distributions and methods, and each and every set of them has their own errors and faults lurking..** *oof..*

So, I started with some resources left and right. A good lesson to learn here is to not use old resources. Sort by modern resources, as they don't use utterly outdated ui's.

But whats this? I can install jekyll via apt-get, but also via something called gem, which apparently uses this thing called ruby, but there's also a devkit version and an installer, *"you just have to pick the one that you need"*?

Screw you man! How the heck am I supposed to know which one I need?

## My First Lesson

After installing jekyll with two different methods, both not resulting in the expected outcome (refer to the title of this entry), I stumbled upon the holy grail of resources:

**OFFICIAL DOCUMENTATION.**

This is technically a duplicate entry from Entry1, but I wanted to provide some extra first-hand experience.
It turns out that Jekyll has an [official installation guide](https://jekyllrb.com/docs/installation/ubuntu/), which, and you wouldn't believe it, literally only has like 3 steps! Amazing!

And best of all, **this worked**. I went through slogs of tutorials that included 10+ step sequences on how to install jekyll, and all this time the source of the software had the right idea all along.

So yeah, check official installation guides first *(as if that wasn't obvious to begin with..)*
